The Markets We Trade

Why The Currency Market For Bebvest?

Before we get into what markets we trade let’s look at the typical investor. The majority of investors buy stocks, as they are perceived to be “the go to” market place. Most investors get hooked into the STORY or HYPE of the Stock and fall prey to emotional attachment even if the Stock does not perform. Investors tend to become reliant on GURUS to guide their buying decisions. We often hear investors buying “X” number shares without knowing why, other than it sounded like a good idea at the time or bought as much as they could with the capital at hand. In addition to that most investors choose the usual stocks which are often range bound rather than the growth stocks, which are not often spotted until they are already soaring luring would be investors into buying at the top of the trend with little wriggle room for growth.

The market is divided into 4 broad categories:

  • Stocks
  • Options
  • Futures
  • Currencies
The Stock market is the most spoken about on investing and news shows, so it’s understandable that most investors choose that vehicle, whereas the other 3 are more difficult to understand and commentary on them is rare or non-existent. Stocks are notorious for big unexpected moves and subject to loads of volatility on sporadic news events or comments from “big” named players in the market.

We at Bebvest have chosen to trade only in the Currency market and on selected currencies only.

  • The cost of the trade is extremely low
  • Never an issue on liquidity
  • The least volatile of all the markets (High leverage in the Currency market gives one the illusion of high volatility, yet it's overtrading that causes risky trading not volatility)
  • The Currency market dwarfs all other markets in terms of volume traded, so it is far less susceptible to market manipulation or government intervention
  • The main currency pairs and crosses provide a stable environment, which is where our system is focused
  • The Currency market is very much a cyclical market vs. the stock market, which is generally a growth market, allowing us to trade in ranges
  • The cyclical nature of the Currency market allows us to short the market, whereas the stock market is predominantly a buyer's market, which precludes shorting it for most investors unless you know how to trade Options to take advantage of bear markets
